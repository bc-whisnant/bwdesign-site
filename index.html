Frequently Asked Questions

This is a collection of questions I commonly get asked from other developers or individuals that are aspiring to become a deveolper.

Do you have to go to college to become a developer?

You don't have to get a degree or anything to be a developer. I taught myself to code and never actually went to college. This field is more about experience and capability than anything else.

Why do you use Linux and macOS instead of Windows?

I actually use Windows at work because that is what the pc they gave me has on it. I prefer mac or linux because of the extra security and believe the look of those operating systems is far better than Windows. Before I learned how to code I was an IT professional for about three years. In that time I got tired of windows and decided to make a switch. Also, some programs like Sketch App are not available for Windows so I use my macbook for that. In all reality I can code on a Dell from 2005 running Linux and be completely happy. Having a mac is just a perk of the job.

What are your goals for the future?

I plan on being good at multiple kinds of development. Professionally I am moving more toward a software developer role using Python currently. However, I will always work with websites even if it is just something I do in my spare time.
